First, phosphate in the soil forms phosphate rock through geologic uplift. The soil also transfers phosphorous to plants. Animals eat these plants and consume the phosphorus, which in turn goes back to the soil through decomposition.

Humans mine into the soil, which helps create the chemical reactions in the soil needed to create phosphate fertilizers. When the fertilizer is used in farms, the fertilizer runs off into the water, creating dissolved phosphorus in the water.

Sediments from the water containing phosphorus deposit back on the soil and get absorbed into the soil, and the process repeats and forms a cycle.
